About

Li Lingna is a PhD researcher in Sociology of Education at Universiti Pendidikan Sultan Idris (Malaysia). She holds a Master's degree in Curriculum and Instruction (English) from Northwest Normal University.

Before pursuing doctoral studies, she worked within the Qingyang Municipal Education System in China and has extensive teaching experience across primary school, middle school, and high school levels. Her professional career spans the full spectrum of basic education, particularly in rural educational contexts.

She is also a TEM-8 certified English major. Her academic interests focus on sociocognitive perspectives in language learning, cross-cultural cognition, and structural deviations in English learning.

Research Interests

  • Sociocognitive perspectives on language learning
  • Structural deviations in English learning
  • Cross-cultural cognition and language
  • Multicultural and intercultural education
  • Language structure and cognitive patterns

International Academic Visits

Li Lingna has participated in academic visits, research exchanges, and international academic activities in various countries and regions, including:

  • Herzen State Pedagogical University of Russia
  • Universities in Dubai and several institutions across the Middle East
  • Universities in Singapore
  • Universities in Hong Kong
  • Academic visits to universities in Europe

These international experiences have provided her with broad perspectives on global education systems and intercultural learning.

Research Projects

  • Principal Investigator of a Provincial-level Education Research Project focusing on English education and cognitive differences in language learning.
  • Participant in multiple educational research projects related to curriculum development, language teaching innovation, and AI-assisted language learning.
  • Research on structural deviations in English learning from a sociocognitive perspective.
  • Research on AI-assisted English teaching and learning tools in primary education.
