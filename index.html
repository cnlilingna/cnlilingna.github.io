About

Li Lingna is a PhD researcher in Sociology of Education at Universiti Pendidikan Sultan Idris (Malaysia). She received her Master's degree in Curriculum and Instruction (English) from Northwest Normal University in China.

Before pursuing doctoral studies, she worked within the Qingyang Municipal Education System and accumulated extensive teaching experience across the full spectrum of basic education, including primary school, middle school, and high school English teaching. Her professional experience spans both rural and urban educational contexts.

Her academic work focuses on sociocognitive perspectives in language learning, cross-cultural cognition, and structural deviations in English learning. She is particularly interested in how language structure reflects cognitive patterns across cultures and how these differences influence second language acquisition.

Through her research and educational practice, she aims to bridge classroom teaching experience with theoretical insights from sociocognitive linguistics and multicultural education.
